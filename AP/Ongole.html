Historical Sites Around Ongole

 

Chennakesava Swamy Temple

The temple of Chenna Kesava Swami (Lord Vishnu) was constructed in the 18th century. The temple is devoted to Lord Vishnu, where an idol of him with his consort, Goddess Lakshmi,can be found.

The temple is known for its architectural and religious importance. In the Kalayana Mandapam there are 6 musical pillars that produce the 7 musical notes when tapped. It is 131.4 kilometers from ongole.

Visiting Timings:-6:00AM-8:30PM

 

Chandravaram

Located at 78 kilometers from ongole, a major Buddhist site has been excavated here on a hillock known locally as Singarakonda, beside the Gundlakamma rivulet. A unique double terraced stupa on the hilltop, perhaps the only one of its kind in South India, is reminiscent of the famous Dharajaka stupa.

A museum at the site houses important sculptures and other relics such as coins, black and red ware and inscriptions in the Brahmi script dating back to the 2nd century B.C.

Visiting Timings:-9:00AM-6:00PM

 

Bhairavakona

Located 64 km from Kanigiri & 145.8 kilometers from ongole, the remains of several rock-cut temples dedicated to Shiva and other Hindu deities, dating back to the 7th & 8th centuries A.D., can be seen here. The shrines have lingas and small images of Shiva as well as other Gods.

Visiting Timings:-7:30AM-6:00PM
