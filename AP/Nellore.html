Historical Sites Around Nellore

 

Udayagiri Fort

Originally built in the 17th century, the fort was rebuilt by Maharaja Marthanda Varma of Travancore in the 18th century.The fort was rebuilt during the reign of Marthanda Varma, between 1741-44 under the supervision of Eustachius De Lannoy, a Flemish naval commander of the Dutch East India Company, who later served as the Chief of the Travancore Army.

In the early days, the fort was of strategic importance. Prisoners captured in the campaign against Tippu Sultan were confined in the fort for some time. In 1810, the East India Company's Army under Colonel Leger marched into Travancore through the Aramboly Pass (Aralvaimozhi) to quell a rebellion under the leadership of Velu Thambi Dalava.

In later years, English East India Company troops were stationed at the fort until the middle of the 19th century. A foundry for the manufacture of guns, mortars and cannonballs was established within the fort under the supervision of the resident General.It is 106 kilometers from Nellore.

Visiting Timings:-8:00AM-5:00PM

 

Venkatagiri Fort

Venkatagiri fort (Venkatagiri Durgam) was constructed in the year 1775 AD and is quite popular with the tourists who visit the fort to witness its magnificence and splendour. The front part of the fort is called Indra Mahal, which looks really appealing.The fort is ideal for those who have an interest in history and visiting historic spots.

The photographers will also find the place very exciting as they can get good snaps of the fort and the surrounding area which is very picturesque. Venkatagiri fort is like a landmark of Venkatagiri village. It is 80.7 kilometers from Nellore.

Visiting Timings:-10:00AM-4:30PM

 

Jonnawada Temple

One of the popular pilgrim centres of India, is Jonnawada, which is 14.1 kilometers from Nellore. It is famous for one of the two Kamakshi temples located here. The main Kamakshi temple is said to have been built here, in the year 1150 A.D. and consists of shrines dedicated to Sri Mallikarjuna Swamy and Goddess Kamakshamma.

The temple is popular for the 10-day festival that is held in dedication to Goddess Kamakshi, who is considered to be an incarnation of Shakti. Some of the parts of the temple like the Gopuram of the temple are made out of a single stone.

Some unexplored and undiscovered villages are located on the way to this temple. People who come here to pray to the goddess, believe that it will help them stay away from serious diseases and illness.

Visiting Timings:-7:00AM-1:00PM and 5:00PM-9:00PM

 

Ranganatha Temple

The Ranganatha Temple is located on the banks of the Pennar river and is also considered to be one of the most visited ancient temples present in Nellore. There are around seven gold pots within the temple, along with large mirrors. This temple with a 29 m high entrance, is said to be about 600 years old.

Devotees come here in large numbers, especially to attend the Rath Yatra festival every year. Inside the sanctum, the main deity by the name of Garbhodakasayi Vishnu rests on a seat called the Anantha Shesha.

On the chest of the idol, is a lotus flower on which Goddess Lakshmi is seated. The temple also houses an idolof a four handed incarnation of Goddess Lakshmi, named as Ranganayaki Devi. This temple is believed to have been built in 1070 A.D. It is 2.6 kilometers from Nellore.

Visiting Timings:-9:00AM-12:00PM and 2:00PM-9:00PM
