Historical Sites Around Srikakulam

 

Srimukhalingeswara Temple

Srimukhalingeswara temple is considered to be a part of a group of three temples, devoted to the three different forms of Lord Shiva, namely Bhimeswara, Someswara and Mukhalingeswara.The temple is situated on the shores of the Vamsadhara River.

It has various sculptures, carvings and beautiful architecture that reflect the Indo-Aryan architectural style.The arched gate of the temple is adorned with statues of lions on each side. The first gate of the temple leads to the outer prakara and a mandapam. Another carved gate leads to the inner prakara where the main shrine is located.

The temple complex has various shrines dedicated to Lord Shiva and Lord Ganesha.A finely carved temple is situated in the middle of the inner prakara. Various images of gods and goddesses are carved on the walls of the temple. Special pujas and religious ceremonies are organised on the occasion of Mahashivratri.It is 48.2 kilometers away from srikakulam.

Visiting Timings:-9:00AM-7:00PM

 

Srikurmanatha Temple

Srikurmanatha temple is one of the ancient temples devoted to Lord Vishnu in the Srikurmam village. Lord Kurmam is considered as one of the forms of Lord Vishnu and is also called as Lord Kurmanath. It is believed to be the only temple devoted to Lord Vishnu in the form of Kurmavatara, the tortoise incarnation, in the country.

The temple pillars are adorned with carvings and inscriptions dating from the 11th century to the 19th century, in Devanagari script. Various paintings and a five-storeyed gopuram are some of the attractions of the temple.

The sanctum is lighted with lamps and can be reached through a narrow passage. A long platform of stone, along with a small platform, resembles the one in the temple of Panakala Narasimha Swamy at Mangalagiri.It is 27.8 kilometers away from srikakulam.

Visiting Timings:-6:00AM-7:45PM

 

Sangam

Sangam village is considered as one of the popular pilgrimage centres located in the Srikakulam District. The village is located near the confluence of the Nagavali, Suvarnamukhi and Vegavati rivers. This triveni sangam resembles the triveni sangam in Allahabad.

Another attraction of this place is the Sangameswara Swamy temple that has a lingam of Lord Sangameswara. The shrine is one of the five linga kshetras and is popular for the grand celebrations of Mahashivratri.It is 64 kilometers away from srikakulam.

Visiting Timings:-9:00AM-9:00PM

 

Mandasa

Mandasa village is situated on the foothills of the Mahendragiri hills. The place is popular for the fort which is believed to have been the residential place of the erstwhile landlords of Mandasa.

It is believed to have been constructed in 1779 by Lakshmana Raja Mani Deo and has several fortifications that are decorated with beautiful paintings and carvings.The Vasudevamurthy Alayam temple is another popular attraction of this place, which is believed to have been constructed by Raja Harihara Mani Deo, one of the landlords of Mandasa.

The idol of the presiding deity is carved from a red sand rock. Tourists also visit the other prominent religious attraction, the Varahaswamy temple.It is 98.4 kilometers from srikakulam.

Visiting Timings:-6:00AM-9:00PM

 

Tarlakota

Tarlakota, surrounded by hills and green fields, is popular for its beautiful temples. The Jagannadha Swamy temple is famous for its typical Odissi architectural style. The 11th century three storeyed Tarlakota Palace is believed to be constructed on a platform and is famous for its huge central hall.

A beautiful mandapam and an archway are some of the major attractions of this palace. The stone carved Balaji temple is another attraction of Tarlakota that resembles the architecture of the Radhagovindaswamy temple of Meliaputti. It is 82.9 kilometers from srikakulam.

Visiting Timings:-9:00AM-6:00PM

 

Surangiraja Palace

Ichchapuram town is famous for the Surangiraja Palace, which has beautiful carvings on its pillars and front elevation.

There are various paintings on the inner fortification of this palace.It is a magnificent heritage building. Walls of the building are adorned with beautiful paintings describing aesthetic values of the bygone era. It is 130 kilometers from srikakulam

Visiting Timings:-10:00AM-5:00PM
