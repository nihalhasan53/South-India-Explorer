Historical Sites Around Khammam

 

Khammam Fort

The Khammam Fort, situated on the Stambhadri hill, is believed to have been constructed in 950 AD by the rulers of the Kakatiya dynasty. It is at distance of 1.5 km from Khammam Railway Station.

The fort was reconstructed and renovated by many rulers, amongst which the Musunuri Nayaks, Velama kings and rulers of the Qutub Shahi kingdom played a vital role in developing several parts of the fort. Both Hindu and Muslim influences can be seen in the architecture of this fort.

Visiting Timings:-10:00AM-5:00PM

 

Narasimha Swamy Temple

This is the tapobhoomi of great sage moudgalya where he did severe penance for lord Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy and was blessed by lord himself.

Kakatya rulers constructed a temple here in 16th century.Monthly celebrations like Rathotsava on Swathi Nakshatra Annual celebrations like Bramothsava, Garudoutsva, Special Months celebrations are held.It is exactly 16 kilometers away from khammam.

Visiting Timings:-6:00AM-10:00PM

 

Parnasala

Parnasala is believed to be the site where Lord Rama spent many years of his exile along with his brother and consort. Owing to this mythological heritage, this site is considered sacred among Hindus.It is 150 kilometers away from khammam.It can be reached using bus transport easily.

One of the prime attractions of this site is the Radhagutta which is believed to be the place where Sita dried her clothes after taking bath in the nearby stream. Legends state that at this site Lord Rama killed the demon Mareecha, who came disguised as a golden deer to deceive Sita.

There is a hillock here, where it is believed that the demon king Ravana parked his vehicle named pushpaka. Sri Venkateshwara Swamy temple, also known as the Telangana Tirupati, is situated in Jamalapuram

Visiting Timings:-8:00AM-6:00PM

 

Jamalapuram Telangana Tirupati

Sri Venkateshwara Swamy temple, also known as the Telangana Tirupati, is situated in Jamalapuram.The presiding deity in this temple is Lord Balaji and is said to be a swayambhu Lord, who self-manifested in this place. Since it is a swayambhoo temple, this temple seems to have been in existence from thousands of years.

It was renovated by Sri Krishnadevaraya, the emperor of Vijayanagara Empire. The temple is located in a serene pleasant ambience surrounded by lush green hills. Sri Venkateshwara Swamy Temple located in Jamalapuram is famously known as Telangana Tirupathi.

The legend has it that in ancient days Jabali Maharshi worshipped and did penance here at a place called Suchi Gutta and was said to have been blessed by Lord Venkateshwara. The temple has sub-shrines for Padmavathi Ammavaru, Sri Alamelu Ammavaru, Lord Shiva, Lord Ganesh, Lord Ayyappa, and Lord Anjaneya.It is 80 kilometers away from khammam.

Visiting Timings:-9:00AM-10:00PM
